Boot Care & Longevity

Protect your investment. Learn the exact cleaning, drying, and storage protocols to maintain material structural integrity.

Phase 1: Post-Match Decontamination

How to safely remove pitch debris without breaking down upper synthetics and glues.

🧹

Dry Soil Removal

Always let heavy mud dry slightly post-match, then clap the soleplates together to dislodge bulk crust. Use a soft-bristled toothbrush to gently clear remaining packed dirt from individual chevron and conical studs.

🧼

Upper Textile Wipe

Mix lukewarm water with a drop of mild dish soap. Dip a microfiber cloth into the solution and wipe down synthetic or knit uppers (like Gripknit or Vaporposite+). Avoid harsh chemical solvents that can degrade graphic overlays.

⚠️

The Golden Machine Rule

Never put your premium boots in a washing machine or spin dryer. The high rotational forces, abrasive drum contact, and soaking heat will permanently warp soleplate plastic and dissolve the bonding adhesives.

Phase 2: Thermal Moisture Evacuation

Moisture control mechanics engineered to stop bacteria and prevent upper hardening.

📰

The Newspaper Core Method

Remove internal insoles completely to expose the lower lasting board. Stuff the inside tightly with standard un-inked paper towels or plain old newspaper. This draws internal sweat and moisture outward through capillary action.

🚫

Zero Direct Heat Exposure

Keep wet hardware far away from radiators, hair dryers, or blazing direct afternoon sunlight. Intense, localized heat makes premium synthetic elements brittle and hardens natural K-leather into a cracked layer.
